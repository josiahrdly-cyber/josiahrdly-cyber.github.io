PC Hardware Upgrade — HDD to NVMe SSD Migration with OS Cloning

← Back to Projects

The Problem

My laptop struggled to run multiple applications at once — especially Adobe Creative Cloud programs alongside other software. Using Task Manager to diagnose the slowdown, I found the hard drive sitting at or near 100% utilization: the aging HDD was the bottleneck, not the CPU or RAM.

The Plan

Rather than a fresh Windows install, I chose to clone the existing OS and files to a new drive to preserve my full working environment — installed programs, settings, and licenses — with zero reconfiguration.

Hardware selected:

  • Samsung 990 EVO Plus 1TB NVMe SSD (M.2 2280, PCIe Gen 4x4, up to 7,250 MB/s read speeds)
  • Sabrent USB 3.2 Type-C tool-free M.2 NVMe enclosure — used to connect the new SSD externally during the cloning process

The Process

  1. Installed the 990 EVO Plus into the Sabrent enclosure and connected it to the laptop over USB 3.2.
  2. Used Samsung Magician's cloning tool to migrate the entire OS, applications, and data from the internal HDD to the SSD.
  3. After verifying the clone completed successfully, opened the laptop and physically swapped the drives, installing the SSD internally.

Verification

  • Entered BIOS to confirm the system detected the new SSD and booted from it as the primary drive.
  • Booted into Windows and confirmed all applications, files, and settings carried over intact.
  • Checked drive health and firmware status in Samsung Magician.

Results

The bottleneck is gone. The laptop now runs multiple programs simultaneously — including Adobe applications — without slowdowns, and overall responsiveness (boot, app launches, file operations) improved dramatically. Task Manager no longer shows disk utilization maxing out under normal multitasking.
